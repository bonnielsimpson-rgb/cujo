Rabies Exposure Decision Assistant

A branching, one-question-at-a-time tool for rabies exposure decisions. This is a decision-support aid based on the Oregon human and animal rabies algorithms. It is not a substitute for clinical judgment, local public health direction, or the full guidance.

Do not enter names, dates of birth, addresses, medical record numbers, or any other identifying information into this tool.

Helpful reminders

Provoked usually means there was a clear reason for the bite: handling, feeding, cornering, caging, crowding space, or similar. Think normal animal behavior, not necessarily good animal behavior.

Quarantine here means a 10-day observation period for a dog, cat, or ferret after a human bite.
